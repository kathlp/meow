About PAWS

History

The Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS) boasts a rich history that stretches back to 1954. Founded by Muriel Jay, a British educator with a fierce love for animals, PAWS emerged from a growing concern about the rampant animal cruelty and suffering in the Philippines. In its early years, PAWS functioned with a shoestring budget, relying on the resourcefulness and dedication of its founders. They raised funds through creative means like selling handmade crafts, laying the groundwork for a future fueled by passionate volunteers and generous donors. Despite a period of dormancy in the late 1970s, PAWS reemerged with renewed vigor in the 1980s under the leadership of dedicated individuals like Atty. Antonio "Tony" La Vina. This marked the beginning of PAWS' rise to prominence as a leading voice for animal welfare in the Philippines.

Nature

As a registered non-profit organization, PAWS thrives on the unwavering dedication and passion of its volunteers and donors. These remarkable individuals form the backbone of the organization, fueling its vital work through countless hours of service and generous financial contributions. PAWS prioritizes a multifaceted approach to animal welfare, recognizing that true progress requires a combination of direct action, legislative changes, and cultural shifts. Their activities encompass animal rescue and rehabilitation, passionate advocacy for stronger animal protection laws, and educational initiatives aimed at fostering responsible pet ownership.

This multi-pronged strategy positions PAWS as a powerful force for positive change in the Philippines. Their rescue and rehabilitation efforts provide a safe haven for animals who have been victims of cruelty, neglect, or abandonment. Through their advocacy work, PAWS has played a pivotal role in shaping the legal landscape for animal welfare in the Philippines. Their instrumental role in the creation of Republic Act 8485, also known as the Animal Welfare Act of 1998, stands as a testament to their unwavering advocacy.

Mission

The mission of PAWS is clear: to alleviate animal suffering and promote a more humane society in the Philippines. They strive to achieve this mission through several key objectives:

  • Rescue and Rehabilitation: PAWS provides a safe haven for animals who have been victims of cruelty, neglect, or abandonment.
  • Animal Welfare Legislation: PAWS actively advocates for stronger animal protection laws and policies. Their instrumental role in the creation of the Animal Welfare Act of 1998 stands as a testament to their dedication.
  • Responsible Pet Ownership: PAWS recognizes the importance of educating pet owners on proper care and responsible pet practices. Through educational initiatives, they empower Filipinos to become better companions to their animal friends.
  • Population Control: PAWS tackles the issue of pet overpopulation through the provision of affordable neutering and spaying programs. This helps to reduce the number of animals ending up homeless or in shelters.

By tackling animal welfare on multiple fronts, PAWS plays a vital role in creating a more compassionate and responsible Philippines for all creatures.
