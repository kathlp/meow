PAWS: Champions for Animal Welfare in the Philippines

The Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS), established in 1954, stands as a pillar of animal welfare in the Philippines. This registered non-profit organization has a rich history of advocating for humane treatment of all creatures, great and small. PAWS played a critical role in the creation of Republic Act 8485, also known as the Animal Welfare Act of 1998, which provides important legal protections for animals in the Philippines. Their work goes far beyond legislation, though. PAWS operates a shelter for animals rescued from cruelty or neglect, offering them medical care, rehabilitation, and a chance at finding loving forever homes. Additionally, PAWS recognizes that prevention is key. They run low-cost neutering and spaying programs to help control the pet population and reduce the number of animals ending up homeless. Through education and outreach efforts, PAWS empowers Filipino citizens to become responsible pet owners and fosters a culture of compassion towards animals throughout the country.
